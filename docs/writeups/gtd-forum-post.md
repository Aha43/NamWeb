**Subject: NamWeb — the GTD-inspired org app I wanted, now on the web (free demo, no login)**

Hi all,

GTD has shaped how I work for years, and at some point I stopped looking for the perfect app and just built the one *I* wanted. This is **NamWeb** — posting it here on the off chance some of you like the direction I took it.

The short version: it's built around **capture → clarify → focus**, but the thing I leaned into is treating your whole workspace as one tree — projects, sub-projects, and actions, with tags, due dates, and prerequisites — and then a **Focus** mode that walks you through it one card at a time. It runs in the browser, so phone, tablet, or laptop, nothing to install.

One thing worth knowing before you try it: **the experience is quite different on a phone vs. a browser on a PC/Mac.** On a phone it's lean and capture-first — quick in, jot it down, get out. On a big screen it opens up into a much fuller workbench for real triage and project work. So if the mobile version feels minimal, that's by design — try it on a desktop browser too to see the whole thing.

**Three ways to try it, depending on how serious you want to get:**

1. **Just kick the tires — no account needed.** A no-login demo with a pre-populated workspace and a reset button, so you can click around freely:
   👉 https://usenam.app/demo

2. **Try it in earnest — make an account.** Sign up and go if you want to keep your own stuff across devices. (There's a companion desktop app, NamDesktop, and the two sync through a shared backend — but NamWeb stands fully on its own.)

3. **Run it yourself — it's open source (MIT):** https://github.com/Aha43/NamWeb

That last option used to be strictly nerd territory, but these days it's surprisingly approachable — with AI coding agents like Claude Code you can clone a repo and have an agent walk you through getting it running, even if you're not a developer. 😄 (Fun fact: NamWeb itself was built almost entirely by directing Claude Code.)

If any of you give it a spin and have suggestions, I'd be happy to receive them — **arne.halvorsen@gmail.com**. I'd especially love to hear from people who live and breathe GTD: where my take fits your workflow and where it doesn't. Thanks for reading!
